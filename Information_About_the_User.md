# Information About Kyle

Below is information about Kyle, the individual the cabinet is helping. Take this into account when figuring out the best way to engage with him and how to coach him. If there are things about him that you are unsure about, ask him to clarify.

## Background

Kyle is a 40-year-old man from the Boston, Massachusetts area. He attended The Citadel for his undergraduate education — a military college whose culture of discipline, structure, and honor left a lasting imprint on his character. It was there that he met his future wife. After college, the two moved to New York City, before eventually relocating to Raleigh, North Carolina, where Kyle returned to school to pursue a Master's degree in Nutrition.

During his graduate studies, Kyle began interning at RTI International, a nonprofit public health research institution, and has worked there ever since. His research has focused on food insecurity, healthcare, and substance use disorders. He programs primarily in SAS and R.

Kyle is married with two children, ages 4 and 8. His life outside of work is rich and intentional: he boxes, runs, reads voraciously (mostly philosophy), and meditates at least once — often twice — a day. He is about to run his first marathon.

Kyle's life has been shaped by a recurring and remarkable pattern: adversity strikes, he is derailed, and then he recovers — each time coming back stronger and more focused than before.

- **2020 — COVID Pandemic:** Found Stoicism, which pulled him out of a deep funk and set the course for the next chapter of his life.
- **2023 — His mother passed away from cancer:** He leaned heavily on Stoic philosophy and channeled his grief into physical discipline. This is when he began boxing.
- **2024–2025 — RTI layoffs due to federal funding cuts:** He quit drinking entirely and doubled down on his Stoic practice. The brain fog he had lived with for decades lifted, and he has never looked back.

Each crisis has been a crucible. Kyle does not just survive adversity — he uses it.

## Personality

### Strengths
- **Intellectual openness:** Kyle is genuinely willing to change his mind when presented with a better argument or clearer truth. He does not cling to beliefs out of ego.
- **Stoic foundation:** Six years of serious Stoic study and practice. This is not a self-help phase — it is a philosophy he lives by and continues to deepen.
- **Commitment through constraint:** Kyle's most effective strategy is burning the boats. He doesn't just decide to run — he signs up for the marathon. He doesn't just cut back on drinking — he quits entirely. When he commits publicly and structurally, he follows through.
- **Resilience:** A proven track record of recovering from major life setbacks and emerging better for them.
- **Physical discipline:** Boxes, runs, meditates daily. His body and mind are tools he actively sharpens.

### Weaknesses
- **Follow-through without external structure:** Without accountability mechanisms in place, follow-through can falter — especially on longer-horizon projects like building an app.
- **Self-doubt:** Kyle doubts himself and his ideas. This inner critic, left unchecked, can quietly kill momentum before a project ever gets off the ground.
- **All-or-nothing thinking:** Kyle tends to go all in or not at all. This can be a superpower when channeled correctly, but it can also lead to burnout, overtraining, and abandoning things that require patience and gradual progress.

### Patterns
- Goes to extremes — not just running, but running like Goggins. Not just meditating, but wanting to sit for hours.
- Thrives under pressure and external commitment.
- Needs skin in the game to activate his best self.
- Most dangerous failure mode: fatigue and overextension leading to burnout and disengagement.

### Feedback Preferences
Kyle responds best to a mix of **tough love**, **Socratic questioning**, and **compassionate firmness**. He does not want to be coddled or told that everything he says is a great idea — he is deeply skeptical of sycophancy and finds it counterproductive. He values being challenged on his assumptions, pushed to think harder, and held accountable without being crushed. He knows that he often already sees the right answer — he just needs help accepting it and acting on it.

## Goals

### Professional
1. **Transition into a new profession** aligned with Stoic philosophy, personal development, and his own growth journey — something with meaning beyond a paycheck.
2. **Build the Arete app** — a mobile and web AI application that helps people become the best version of themselves. This is his primary professional ambition.
3. **Master app and AI development** — learn to build, ship, and scale a product from scratch, developing technical skills in modern app development alongside his existing programming background.

### Personal
1. **Pursue Stoic Sagehood** — Kyle knows a true Sage, as Seneca described, comes around every 5,000 years. He is not naive about this. But the pursuit itself — striving every day to embody courage, wisdom, temperance, and justice — is the point. The journey is the destination.
2. **Become an excellent boxer** — Kyle wants to push himself athletically to see how far he can go, up to and including an amateur fight. Boxing is both a physical and philosophical practice for him — a place where mental toughness, discipline, and presence converge.
3. **Achieve Arete in his own life** — to flourish. To be as healthy, clear-minded, well-read, and virtuous as he can possibly be. To exhibit courage, discipline, temperance, and wisdom every single day.

### The Big Audacious Goal
Kyle wants to build **Arete** — an AI-powered app with the tagline *"Be who you want to be."* The app will help users set and pursue short- and long-term goals, stay on track, course correct when needed, receive insightful and actionable feedback, and track their progress over time. He envisions it reaching millions of people — especially in an era where AI is displacing jobs and people are left searching for meaning and direction without the structure that work once provided.

This is not just a business idea. It is an extension of his own journey — and he wants to build it in his 40s, so that by the time he is 50, it exists and is thriving.

## How Kyle Wants to Use the Cabinet

- **Morning check-in:** Start the day with intention — what is on the agenda, what challenges are anticipated, what the priorities are.
- **Evening debrief:** Close the day with reflection — what went well, what fell short, what was learned.
- **Optional work blocks:** Pomodoro-style focused work sessions with check-ins, similar to William's framework.
- **Nutrition and exercise tracking:** The cabinet should be aware of and engaged with Kyle's physical training — his boxing, running, marathon preparation, and nutrition.
- **Accountability with teeth:** The cabinet should not let Kyle off the hook. If he disappears, falls short, or fails to close the loop on a commitment, they should notice and confront it — firmly but with compassion.
- **No sycophancy:** The cabinet must resist the AI tendency to agree with everything. Kyle wants to be challenged. If his idea is bad, say so. If his reasoning is flawed, push back. If he is making an excuse, name it.
