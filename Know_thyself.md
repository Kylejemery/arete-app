🧠 Personality
4.	How would you describe yourself? (strengths, weaknesses, patterns you notice)
Strengths – Willing to change my mind when shown the correct way, I have a strong background in Stoicism, I have been studying the philosophy for the last six years. 
Weaknesses - Follow through can be a weakness, but I do best when I put myself in a position where I have to follow through, like a "burn all the boats" strategy.  For example, I wanted to improve my cardio and running ability, so instead of just running I signed up for a half marathon last fall, followed by a full marathon in two weeks.  Another weakness, I doubt myself and my ideas, which I think adds to lack of follow through. 
Patterns - I tend to go all in, bring everything to the max.  I don't just run, I want to run like David Goggins  Or I don't just meditate, I want to be able to meditate for hours. I'm very all or nothing, which can be a strength when the goal is perfect moderation.  
5.	What are your biggest failure modes — what tends to derail you or hold you back?
Failure modes, I get derailed and held back when I’m tired.  So, I always try prioritizing sleep and rest.  I often like to overtrain, which sometimes leads to burnout.  Large, stressful life events have also derailed my progress, including 2020 Covid, my mother passing away from Cancer in 2023, and the Trump administration decimating funding for public health that led to major layoffs at my company.  Good news, in the wake of each of those events and following a period of derailment, I’ve recovered for the better.  First in 2020, finding Stoicism helped me get out of a Pandemic “funk.”  After my mother died, again, I leaned heavily on Stoicism and being healthier, this is when I began boxing.  Lastly, after my last derailment period, I quit drinking completely and doubled down on my Stoic practice.    
6.	What kind of feedback works best for you — tough love, nurturing support, Socratic questioning, or a mix?
A mix, but also Socratic questioning and tough love work well.  I think often in life you know and see the answer you’re looking for, but it always takes time to realize and accept the answer.  I also value questioning assumptions I have had for a long time.  One example would be consuming alcohol. I always assumed that alcohol was a part of any lifestyle, not until I quit drinking for more than a month did, I realize that alcohol holds you back in almost any facet of life.  I also did not realize that I had been in a brain fog for the last three decades.
